Neurologist

A neurology department is a medical unit within a hospital or healthcare facility that specializes in the diagnosis and treatment of disorders related to the nervous system. Neurologists, who are medical doctors with expertise in neurology, are the primary healthcare professionals in this department.

Ophthalmologist

An ophthalmologist is a medical doctor specialized in eye and vision care. They diagnose and treat eye diseases, perform eye surgeries, prescribe corrective lenses, and provide comprehensive eye care.

Orthopedic

Orthopedic pertains to the medical field dealing with conditions and injuries affecting the bones, joints, muscles, and related structures. It involves diagnosis, treatment, and prevention methods like medication, therapy, and surgery to enhance musculoskeletal health and function.

Gastroenterologist

A gastroenterologist is a doctor who specializes in treating disorders of the digestive system, including the stomach, intestines, liver, and pancreas. They diagnose and manage conditions like acid reflux, irritable bowel syndrome, and liver diseases using procedures like endoscopy and imaging tests.

A urologist is a doctor specializing in urinary tract and male reproductive system issues. They diagnose and treat conditions like infections, kidney stones, and prostate problems through surgery, medication, and lifestyle advice.

Services

CT-Scan

CT scans are powerful diagnostic tools that play a crucial role in modern medicine, aiding in the early detection and treatment of various medical conditions. However, due to the use of radiation, they are used judiciously and in accordance with clinical necessity. Patients should discuss the risks and benefits of a CT scan with their healthcare provider before undergoing the procedure.

X-Ray

X-ray, short for "X-radiation," is a commonly used medical imaging technique that uses electromagnetic radiation to create images of the inside of the body. .

Full Body Checkup

A full body checkup, also known as a comprehensive health checkup or an annual physical examination, is a comprehensive medical assessment aimed at evaluating a person's overall health and identifying any potential health issues or risk.

Dialysis

Dialysis is a treatment for kidney failure, removing waste and excess fluid from the blood when the kidneys can't. It's essential for maintaining electrolyte balance and managing fluid levels in the body.

Electrocardiography

Electrocardiography (ECG) is a diagnostic tool that records the heart's electrical activity through electrodes placed on the skin. It is used to diagnose various heart conditions such as arrhythmias and heart attacks, providing crucial insights into heart health and guiding treatment decisions.

Blood banks

Blood banks store and provide blood for transfusions. They collect blood donations, test them for safety, and distribute blood products to hospitals for patients in need of transfusions due to surgery, trauma, or medical conditions.
